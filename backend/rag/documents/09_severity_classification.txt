INCIDENT SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION

SEVERITY TIERS:

TIER 1 — LOW:
- Single vehicle, minor property damage, no injuries
- One lane partially blocked, traffic can flow around
- Expected duration: 5-15 minutes
- Response: Signal adjustments only, no diversion needed
- Staffing: Automated recommendations, officer monitoring

TIER 2 — MEDIUM:
- Two vehicles, minor injuries, right lane(s) blocked
- Traffic significantly slowed but not stopped
- Expected duration: 15-30 minutes
- Response: Signal re-timing + single diversion route + VMS alerts
- Staffing: Officer actively managing, EMS may be on scene

TIER 3 — HIGH:
- Multiple vehicles, confirmed injuries, multiple lanes blocked
- Traffic queuing rapidly upstream
- Expected duration: 30-60 minutes
- Response: Full agent activation (signal + routing + alerts + density monitoring)
- Staffing: NYPD traffic unit on scene, possible road closure

TIER 4 — CRITICAL:
- Major pile-up, severe injuries/fatalities, all lanes blocked
- Complete road closure required
- Expected duration: 60+ minutes
- Response: Maximum response — all agents, multiple diversions, media coordination
- Staffing: NYPD command post, OEM notification, Borough President notification

ESCALATION CRITERIA:
- Escalate by +1 tier if: Duration exceeds estimate by 50%
- Escalate by +1 tier if: Secondary incident on diversion route
- Escalate by +1 tier if: Cascade risk exceeds 0.7
- De-escalate by -1 tier if: Lanes reopening and speed recovering

BAYESIAN DURATION MODEL:
Prior probabilities based on NYC DOT historical data:
- P(LOW) = 0.45, mean_duration = 8 min, std = 3 min
- P(MEDIUM) = 0.30, mean_duration = 15 min, std = 5 min
- P(HIGH) = 0.18, mean_duration = 35 min, std = 10 min
- P(CRITICAL) = 0.07, mean_duration = 60 min, std = 20 min

CONSTRUCTION ZONE INCIDENT PROTOCOL

Priority Level: ELEVATED (all incidents in work zones)
Reference: NYC DOT Work Zone Traffic Control Manual

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Reduced lane capacity already in effect
- Worker safety is paramount — any incident near workers escalates by +1 tier
- Temporary traffic patterns may confuse unfamiliar drivers
- Construction equipment may be blocking additional lanes

DETECTION:
- Speed baseline must account for existing construction restrictions
- Anomaly detection threshold adjusted: 25% drop (vs standard 40%)
- Flag any incident within 200m of active work zone

SIGNAL MANAGEMENT:
- Maintain construction-zone signal plans — do not override without approval
- If flaggers are present, coordinate signal changes with flagger operations
- No automated signal changes within active work zone without human confirmation

DIVERSION:
- Do not route diverted traffic through other construction zones
- Account for reduced capacity on work zone approaches
- Coordinate with DOT Contractor for potential work suspension
- Night construction: increased diversion radius (800m vs 600m)

PUBLIC ALERTS:
- VMS: Include "WORK ZONE" in messaging
- Radio: Mention construction zone — drivers already expect delays
- Social media: Tag @NYC_DOT for coordination
- Link to NYC DOT permit database for construction schedule

WORKER SAFETY:
- If incident is within active work zone, PAUSE work immediately
- Ensure workers are behind barriers before allowing any lane changes
- Coordinate with construction supervisor before reopening lanes
- Document worker proximity to incident for OSHA reporting

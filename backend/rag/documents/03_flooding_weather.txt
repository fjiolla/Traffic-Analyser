FLOODING AND WEATHER INCIDENT PROTOCOL

Priority Level: MEDIUM to CRITICAL (depending on water depth)
Applicable: All low-lying Brooklyn areas, especially near Gowanus Canal, Shore Parkway

DETECTION CRITERIA:
- Speed drops across multiple adjacent segments simultaneously
- Risk scores elevating uniformly across a geographic cluster (not point-source)
- Time-of-day does not explain the speed reduction (weather override)

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Identify affected road segments and confirm flooding reports
2. Close affected segments completely (speed factor = 0)
3. Widen diversion radius to 1km (vs standard 600m for collision)
4. Alert emergency services for potential water rescue

SIGNAL MANAGEMENT FOR FLOODING:
- Switch affected intersections to flash mode (red flash = treat as stop sign)
- Extend green on elevated routes to absorb diverted traffic
- Disable pedestrian signals at flooded crosswalks

DIVERSION CONSIDERATIONS:
- Avoid ALL routes through flood-prone areas (use elevation data)
- Prefer routes on elevated ground (e.g., Prospect Park perimeter vs Gowanus)
- Consider complete corridor closure with bypass routing
- Coordinate with NYPD for road closure enforcement

PUBLIC ALERTS:
- VMS: Direct "ROAD CLOSED" messaging (not "delays expected")
- Radio: Include safety warnings about driving through standing water
- Social media: Include NYC 311 contact for road condition reports
- Duration: Update every 15 minutes until water recedes

RECOVERY:
- Roads must be inspected before reopening after flooding
- Speed limits reduced to 15 mph for first hour after reopening
- Monitor for sinkholes or pavement damage reports

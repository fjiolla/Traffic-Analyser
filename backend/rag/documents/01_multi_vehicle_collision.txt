MULTI-VEHICLE COLLISION RESPONSE PROTOCOL

Priority Level: HIGH to CRITICAL
Applicable Area: All Brooklyn arterial roads

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (0-5 minutes):
1. Confirm incident location and number of vehicles involved via sensor data
2. Dispatch NYPD traffic unit and FDNY if injuries reported
3. Activate upstream signal pre-emption within 600m radius
4. Begin drafting VMS, radio, and social media alerts

SIGNAL MANAGEMENT:
- Extend green phase on perpendicular streets to flush traffic away from incident
- Reduce cycle time on incident approach by 30-40%
- Activate pedestrian hold on affected crosswalks
- Coordinate with adjacent intersections to create "green wave" away from scene

DIVERSION PROTOCOL:
- Identify 2-3 alternate routes using risk-weighted pathfinding
- Prefer routes with excess capacity (density < 60 veh/km)
- Avoid school zones during active hours (7:30-8:30, 14:30-15:30)
- Deploy portable VMS at diversion points within 10 minutes

LANE REOPENING CRITERIA:
- Speed on affected segment must recover to >60% of free-flow baseline
- Risk score must drop below 0.5
- No secondary incidents reported on adjacent segments
- Physical debris fully cleared (confirmed by on-scene unit)

DOCUMENTATION:
- Log all signal timing changes with timestamps
- Record diversion activation and deactivation times
- Note response time from detection to first recommendation
- Export timeline for post-incident review

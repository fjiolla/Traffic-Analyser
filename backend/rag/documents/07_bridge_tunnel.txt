BRIDGE AND TUNNEL INCIDENT PROTOCOL

Priority Level: HIGH to CRITICAL
Applicable: Brooklyn Bridge, Manhattan Bridge, Williamsburg Bridge, Brooklyn-Battery Tunnel

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Bridge/tunnel incidents have outsized network impact (limited crossing capacity)
- Diversion must account for extreme bottleneck effects
- Coordinate with TBTA/MTA for tunnel incidents
- Consider impact on BOTH Brooklyn and Manhattan traffic

DETECTION:
- Speed drop on bridge approach segments (within 1km of bridge)
- Speed drop on bridge itself (if sensor data available)
- Cross-reference with TBTA alert feed

SIGNAL MANAGEMENT:
- Reduce green time on bridge approach to meter remaining vehicles
- Extend green on parallel streets to absorb diverted traffic
- Activate "bridge closed" messaging on VMS 2km upstream
- Coordinate with Manhattan-side signal controllers

DIVERSION:
- If Brooklyn Bridge closed: divert to Manhattan Bridge or Williamsburg Bridge
- If multiple bridges affected: activate tunnel routing
- Include subway alternatives in public messaging (MTA coordination)
- Monitor alternate bridge approaches for cascade congestion

PUBLIC ALERTS:
- VMS: "BRIDGE CLOSED" messaging at all decision points before bridge approaches
- Radio: Include transit alternatives (subway lines, ferries)
- Social media: Real-time bridge status with photo if available
- Update every 5 minutes during bridge closure

CAPACITY:
- Each East River bridge carries approximately 100,000 vehicles/day
- Closure of one bridge increases load on remaining by ~30-40%
- Plan for minimum 2-hour diversion in bridge incidents

EMERGENCY VEHICLE CORRIDOR PROTOCOL

Priority Level: CRITICAL
Applicable: All routes to Brooklyn hospitals (Methodist, LICH, Kings County, Maimonides)

OBJECTIVE:
Create clear corridors for emergency vehicles responding to or from incident scenes.
Signal pre-emption takes priority over traffic flow optimization.

SIGNAL PRE-EMPTION RULES:
- Immediately extend green on the emergency vehicle's approach
- Hold red on all conflicting phases for 15-20 seconds
- Resume normal operation 30 seconds after vehicle passes
- Maximum pre-emption duration: 45 seconds per intersection

CORRIDOR IDENTIFICATION:
Priority hospital routes in Brooklyn:
1. Flatbush Ave corridor → NYC Health + Hospitals/Kings County
2. Atlantic Ave corridor → Brooklyn Methodist Hospital
3. Henry St → Long Island College Hospital
4. 48th St corridor → Maimonides Medical Center
5. Bushwick Ave → Wyckoff Heights Medical Center

COORDINATION:
- Signal agent must flag when emergency corridor conflicts with diversion route
- If diversion uses an emergency corridor, reroute diversion immediately
- Emergency vehicles always take priority over traffic optimization

DOCUMENTATION:
- Log pre-emption activation and duration for each intersection
- Record emergency vehicle travel time for performance tracking
- Note any conflicts between pre-emption and active diversions

PEAK HOUR TRAFFIC DIVERSION PROTOCOL

Priority Level: MEDIUM to HIGH
Peak Hours: 08:00-10:30 (AM), 17:30-20:30 (PM)

OVERVIEW:
Incidents during peak hours require accelerated response due to exponential queue growth.
A 5-minute delay in response during peak hours equals approximately 15 minutes of additional clearance time.

PRE-EMPTIVE MEASURES:
- Monitor all arterial speed feeds for drops exceeding 30% below baseline
- Activate signal coordination plans for affected corridors
- Pre-position diversion messaging on VMS signs at major decision points

SIGNAL ADJUSTMENTS DURING PEAK:
- Maximum green extension: 15 seconds beyond normal cycle
- Minimum pedestrian crossing time must be maintained (federal MUTCD requirement)
- Coordinate adjacent intersections in platoons — do not break green wave
- Consider turning movement restrictions to simplify signal phasing

CAPACITY MANAGEMENT:
- Vehicle density threshold for diversion activation: 80 veh/km
- Monitor diversion routes for secondary congestion — cascade risk assessment
- Volume redistribution should not exceed 35% on any single alternate route
- Reassess every 5 minutes and adjust diversion percentage

COMMUNICATION PRIORITY (PEAK HOURS):
1. VMS signs at highway on-ramps (capture pre-trip decisions)
2. Social media / app notifications (15-minute travel time impact)
3. Radio broadcast (commuter stations, every 10 minutes during incident)
4. Coordination with MTA for transit alternatives messaging

METRICS:
- Target response time from detection: <90 seconds
- Target clearance time improvement: 20-30% vs manual coordination
- Track queue length propagation speed for post-incident analysis

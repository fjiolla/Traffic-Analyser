CASCADE RISK MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL

Priority Level: HIGH
Applicable: When diversion routes approach capacity limits

DEFINITION:
Cascade risk occurs when a diversion route itself becomes congested, potentially
creating secondary incidents or gridlock. This is the most dangerous failure mode
of traffic management systems.

DETECTION CRITERIA:
- Diversion route speed drops below 50% of its free-flow baseline
- Diversion route risk score exceeds 0.65
- Vehicle density on diversion route exceeds 70 veh/km
- Multiple 911 calls from diversion route area

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Reduce diversion percentage by 50% (e.g., from 30% to 15%)
2. Activate secondary diversion route
3. Extend signal green on diversion route approaches
4. Issue updated public alerts with new routing guidance

PREVENTION:
- Supervisor node must check diversion route conditions every 30 seconds
- Volume redistribution should NEVER exceed 35% on a single route
- Pre-compute 2 backup diversion routes at initial incident detection
- Monitor aggregate risk across all affected segments, not just incident zone

SECONDARY INCIDENT PROTOCOL:
If a secondary incident occurs on a diversion route:
1. Immediately close the diversion route
2. Activate backup route
3. Escalate severity tier by +1 (e.g., HIGH → CRITICAL)
4. Deploy additional NYPD traffic units to manual control

METRICS:
- Cascade risk threshold: 0.6 (warning), 0.8 (critical)
- Maximum acceptable secondary incident rate: <5% of diversions
- Track cascade risk trend (increasing/stable/decreasing) for predictive alerting

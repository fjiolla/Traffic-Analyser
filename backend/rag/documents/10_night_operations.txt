NIGHT OPERATIONS PROTOCOL

Priority Level: Varies
Active Hours: 22:00-06:00

OVERVIEW:
Night operations differ from daytime in several critical ways:
- Lower traffic volume but higher per-incident severity
- Reduced visibility increases secondary incident risk
- Fewer available NYPD traffic units
- Signal timing is already in lower-volume plans

DETECTION ADJUSTMENTS:
- Sensitivity threshold reduced — smaller speed drops trigger alerts
- Risk score time-of-day component weighted for impaired driving patterns
- Friday/Saturday nights: 40% higher incident rate baseline (DUI patterns)

SIGNAL MANAGEMENT:
- Many intersections already on flash mode (flash yellow)
- If switching to active control, use conservative cycle lengths (90-120s)
- Maintain minimum green times for pedestrian safety
- Visibility-enhanced signal timing (longer amber phases)

DIVERSION:
- Prefer well-lit arterial routes over residential streets
- Avoid dark, narrow streets even if theoretically faster
- Consider reduced speed limits on diversion routes (25 mph max)
- Fewer alternative routes available (lower-classification roads may be less safe)

PUBLIC ALERTS:
- VMS: ALL CAPS, maximum brightness setting
- Radio: Late-night radio stations and navigation app integration
- Social media: May have lower reach; coordinate with NYPD social accounts
- Consider 311 push notifications for registered users in affected area

STAFFING:
- Automated system operates with reduced human oversight
- Escalation threshold lowered — alert supervisor for MEDIUM+ incidents
- On-call NYPD traffic unit contact maintained
